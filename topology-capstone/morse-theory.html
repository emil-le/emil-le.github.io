Chapter 2 Morse theory

Following the flow of thinking, Morse theory study the invariant of topological manifold by studying its differential function. A sensible function can inform a lot about a manifold, so what is consider a sensible function?

Example Consider a unit sphere \(S^2\subset \mathbb{R}^3\): with the function \(f\) that collapse the sphere into the \(z-\)axis: \[ \begin{aligned} S^2 = \{(x,y,z)&\vert x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1\} \\ \\ f\text{ }: &S^2 \longrightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ &(x,y,z)\longmapsto z \end{aligned} \]

2.1 Differentiate Manifold

Definiton If there exists a continuous bijection \(f: \mathcal{X}\to\mathcal{Y}\) between the topological space \(\mathcal{X}\) and \(\mathcal{Y}\), then \(\mathcal{X}\) and \(\mathcal{Y}\) are homeomorphic (denote as \(\mathcal{X}\cong\mathcal{Y}\)) and \(f\) is called a homeomorphism between these spaces. An embedding of \(\mathcal{X}\) in \(\mathcal{Y}\) is a function \(f: \mathcal{X}\to\mathcal{Y}\) that maps \(\mathcal{X}\) homeomorphically to the subspace \(f(\mathcal{X})\) in \(\mathcal{Y}\).

Informally, we can think of a manifold as a topological space (“shape”) that is locally resemblance to an Euclidean space, but not globally. For example, the Earth is an 2-manifold because we perceive the shape of it locally as a flat surface. This provides us a way to quantify the shape or geometric feature of visual data.

Definiton \ An \(n-\)manifold \(\mathcal{M}\) is a \(T_2\) topological space (Hausdorff and with countable basis), where there exists a neighborhood \(U\subseteq \mathcal{M}\) for every \(m\in\mathcal{M}\) such that \(U\) is homeomorphic to an open ball in \(\RR^n\) induced by the Eucledian metric. By Urysohn’s metrization theorem, the \(n-\)manifold \(\MM\) is metricizable.
