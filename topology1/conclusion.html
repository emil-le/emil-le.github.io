Discrete Morse theory and application in topological data analysis.

Chapter 4 Conclusion

To summarize, we have tried to prove several relevant fundamental ideas of Morse theory in the discrete settings. The main takes away from the project is that with a good choice of a (discrete) Morse function, its non-degenerative critical points can inform a lot about the topological structure of a space. This project also provides two examples of how we can utilize the ideas of persistence critical points through sublevel sets filtration for potential applications image segmentation and network classification. For future works, there are many other applications that we can utilize these results from discrete Morse theory. For example, this paper uses the same ideas to simplify and reconstruct the road network, and this paper uses critical points cancellation from discrete Morse theory to improve the computation of zig-zag persistence.
